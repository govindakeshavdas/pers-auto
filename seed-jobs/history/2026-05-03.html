At least 4 YC companies now target "AI employees for trades businesses" — the pattern is validated but the space is crowding fast.
  • AI compliance documentation across verticals: Saphira (hardware safety ISO/UL), Noetic/Fuchsia (hardware FCC/FDA/CE — Run 7), Stilta (patent IP), SchemeFlow (environmental reports — Run 6), Payna (multi-state licensing — Run 6). Each vertical has its own compliance documentation burden, and AI is now capable enough to draft accurate first passes.
  • Revenue optimization AI for underpriced SMBs: Booko (appointment dynamic pricing), Chasi (equipment dealer revenue — Run 7), Tepali (medspa upselling — Run 7). SMBs leave money on the table because they lack the data infrastructure and pricing sophistication of airlines/hotels. AI-native tools can now bring dynamic pricing to any time-based or asset-based business.

Gaps

  • AI dynamic pricing for physical asset rentals (non-appointment): Booko targets time-slot businesses (fitness, salons). But equipment rental (Run 7 gap), storage unit, and RV/boat rental businesses face the SAME perishable-inventory problem — unused days are $0 revenue. Nobody is building "Booko for self-storage" or "Booko for RV rental fleets." A dynamic pricing engine that integrates with rental management software (SiteLink for self-storage, RMS for RV parks) would address a large, fragmented market with the same "20% revenue uplift" proof point.
  • AI for collision repair supplement negotiation: Ressl automates estimates and parts procurement for collision shops. But the most painful and high-value workflow — insurance supplement negotiation — remains manual. After the initial estimate, shops submit supplements (additional damage found during tear-down) and negotiate with insurance adjusters over pricing. This back-and-forth can add $2K-5K per repair and takes days. Nobody is building AI that generates optimized supplement documentation and handles the adjuster negotiation workflow.
  • AI for ADA/accessibility compliance review in architecture: Avoice does general spec/drawing review. But ADA compliance (Americans with Disabilities Act) is a specialized, high-stakes sub-discipline: every commercial building must comply, violations trigger lawsuits, and checking compliance against ADA Standards for Accessible Design is tedious (door widths, ramp slopes, restroom dimensions). Nobody is building a dedicated AI tool for architects that reviews drawings specifically for ADA compliance violations — a narrow, defensible, and high-value niche.
  • AI for agricultural adjuster field reports: Verdex replaces field inspections with satellite data for insurers. But when field visits ARE still needed (complex claims, pre-loss inspections), the adjuster generates a manual report. Nobody is building "Opusense for crop adjusters" — a tool that lets a field adjuster walk a damaged field, speak observations, snap photos, and generate a structured insurance claim report instantly. This is the intersection of Opusense's workflow (Run 6) and Verdex's market.

Bootstrap Viability

  • Dynamic pricing for ONE appointment vertical (Booko space): Best bootstrap target in this run. Booko is horizontal across all time-slot businesses. A bootstrapper should pick ONE vertical and ONE booking platform: e.g., "dynamic pricing for hair salons on Vagaro" or "dynamic pricing for fitness studios on Mindbody." Build the deepest integration for that platform's API, understand that vertical's pricing psychology, and become the default recommendation in that platform's partner ecosystem. At $199/month/location, 60 locations = $12K MRR. The proof point ("20% revenue uplift") sells itself — you can offer a 2-week free trial and let the numbers speak. Access buyers via Vagaro's Facebook groups, salon industry podcasts, and BeautyPros conferences.
  • AI back-office agents for collision repair shops (Ressl space — narrower): Strong bootstrap target for someone with body shop experience. Ressl is horizontal across all trades. A bootstrapper who has worked in or around a collision repair shop should build "the AI estimator for CCCOne/Mitchell users." The collision repair industry uses exactly two SORs (CCCOne and Mitchell), has a standardized estimating process, and the insurance supplement workflow is the highest-value automation target. Charge $500-1K/month per shop. 15 shops = $7.5K-15K MRR. Reach buyers at SEMA, NACE, and through the I-CAR training community.
  • GDPR-compliant AI outbound for ONE EU country + vertical (Venta space): Excellent bootstrap target for someone in the EU. Venta targets the German market. French, Dutch, Nordic, and Southern European markets are wide open for compliant AI SDR tools. Build "AI SDR for French B2B SaaS companies" — handle French language, French anti-spam law (LCEN), French data sources, and integrate with Hubspot/Pipedrive. The physical letter channel that Venta pioneered could be replicated for any EU country. At €500-1K/month per company, 15 companies = €7.5K-15K MRR. Access buyers at French tech events (France Digitale, BPI France ecosystem).
  • AI for ADA compliance review (Avoice space — gap): Promising niche bootstrap target. Every commercial building project in the US needs ADA compliance review. Currently done by ADA consultants ($5K-15K per project) or by architects manually checking drawings. Build an AI tool that analyzes floor plans for ADA violations: door clearances, ramp slopes, restroom dimensions, signage placement, parking ratios. Sell to small architecture firms at $200-400/month. 30 firms = $6K-12K MRR. The accuracy requirements are high but the standards are well-documented (2010 ADA Standards for Accessible Design) and don't change frequently — good for fine-tuning.
  • AI for hardware pre-compliance (Saphira/Noetic space — narrower): Moderate bootstrap target, requires domain knowledge. Saphira and Noetic are both going broad across many standards. A bootstrapper should own ONE standard deeply: "AI for FCC Part 15B pre-compliance for IoT device makers." FCC Part 15 is the most common certification path (every WiFi/Bluetooth device needs it), the testing requirements are well-documented, and IoT startups are accessible via Hacker News and hardware accelerators. Charge $2K-5K per certification cycle. 3-5 certifications/month = $6K-25K MRR.
