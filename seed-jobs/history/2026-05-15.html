  • Regulation-driven compliance creating new AI categories overnight: Cognitio Labs (FSMA 204 food traceability, 2028 deadline), Dili (IRA prevailing wage for energy/construction), Complir (EU ESPR for retail products — Run 11), Fed10 (legislative monitoring — Run 10). The pattern is identical: a new regulation imposes recordkeeping/reporting obligations on thousands of businesses who have no software to meet them. Each regulation creates a 2-3 year compliance window where demand is guaranteed and buyers are panicking. The bootstrapper's playbook: find the regulation, identify the smallest affected business type, build the compliance tool.
  • AI for fragmented fleet/logistics operations — same architecture, different industries: Dayjob (waste management fleets), FleetWorks (freight matching — Run 12), GrazeMate (cattle herding drones — Run 12). Each targets a different type of fleet but the architecture is identical: ingest job list + vehicle locations → optimize routes → handle exceptions in real-time → report. The waste management niche is particularly underserved because of unique constraints (regulatory disposal rules, container types, pickup windows).
  • Short-term rental operations — now a mature AI sub-ecosystem with role-specific unbundling: Trellis (full-stack operations), Besty AI (guest messaging + upselling — Run 11), Hospitable (messaging automation). Each targets a different operations layer. The pattern: STR operators managing 10-200 properties need AI at every step (guest comms, cleaning dispatch, maintenance, pricing, reviews, accounting). No single tool owns it all, creating opportunity for narrow, feature-specific wedges.
  • "Domain expert who ran the business before building software" — strongest traction signal across all runs: Dayjob founders (spent mornings in waste depots for 3 years), Trellis founder (managed 150+ STR listings to $2M revenue), Cohesive founders (ran $50K MRR service business — Run 11), Patientdesk founders (ran dental call center for 2 years — Run 11), Poka Labs CEO (worked in chemical plants on 3 continents — Run 12). Companies founded by domain insiders consistently show faster traction than pure-tech founders entering a new vertical.

Gaps

  • AI for waste hauler bidding/pricing (Dayjob adjacent): Dayjob optimizes daily scheduling. But the step before — pricing a new commercial waste contract — is still done by gut feel. Roll-off dumpster companies, commercial waste haulers, and recycling operators price bids based on estimated haul frequency, disposal costs, and route density, with no data-driven tool. Nobody is building "AI bid calculator for waste haulers" that takes a prospective customer's address, estimates haul frequency from satellite/Google Maps data, calculates route cost based on existing routes, and generates a profitable bid. The buyer is the same fleet owner who buys Dayjob.
  • AI for food recall simulation/mock drills (Cognitio Labs adjacent): FSMA 204 requires companies to conduct mock recalls to test their traceability systems. Nobody is building "AI mock recall simulator" that generates realistic contamination scenarios, tests the company's ability to produce records within 24 hours, and generates a readiness score. The buyer is the QA manager who needs to demonstrate recall readiness to the FDA. This could be a standalone SaaS tool at $99-299/month that doesn't require sensor hardware — just data inputs from existing food safety systems.
  • AI for STR cleaning quality verification (Trellis adjacent): Trellis dispatches cleaners and coordinates turnovers. But verifying that the cleaning was actually done correctly is still manual — some operators use photo checklists, most do nothing. Nobody is building "AI cleaning quality checker for vacation rentals" where the cleaner uploads 5-10 photos after turnover, the AI verifies against a property-specific checklist (bed made, kitchen clean, amenities stocked), and flags issues before the next guest arrives. The buyer is the same 200K+ STR operators served by Besty and Trellis.
  • AI for IRA apprenticeship compliance (Dili adjacent, narrower): Dili covers full prevailing wage + apprenticeship compliance. But the apprenticeship requirement is the most confusing part — contractors must maintain specific apprentice-to-journeyworker ratios and document good-faith efforts. Nobody is building a standalone "apprenticeship compliance tracker for small solar/EV contractors" that monitors ratios, generates good-faith documentation, and alerts when a project falls below requirements. The buyer is the small solar installer (10-50 employees) who doesn't need Dili's full platform but is terrified of losing their IRA tax credits.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI scheduling for one type of waste fleet in one metro (Dayjob space — narrower): Best bootstrap target in this run. Dayjob is going horizontal across all industrial logistics. A bootstrapper should pick ONE waste type (roll-off dumpsters, front-load commercial, residential recycling) and ONE metro (Houston, Phoenix, Atlanta). Build the simplest possible daily scheduler: import tomorrow's jobs from the ERP, optimize routes using Google Maps API, assign drivers. The waste hauler owner evaluates on "does my planner save 30+ minutes per morning?" — immediate, measurable ROI. $200-400/month per fleet × 30 fleets = $6K-12K MRR. Reach via WasteExpo, SWANA local chapters, and waste industry Facebook groups. The waste industry has ~10K companies in the US, nearly all independently owned.
  • FSMA 204 record generator for small food distributors (Cognitio Labs space — software-only wedge): Regulation-driven demand with a hard deadline. 60K+ food facilities must comply by July 2028. Most small distributors (5-20 employees) don't know where to start. Build a tool where a distributor uploads their shipping documents (BOLs, invoices, receiving logs), the AI extracts KDEs (lot codes, dates, locations), and generates FSMA 204-compliant records in a sortable electronic format. No sensors needed. $200-400/month per facility × 30 facilities = $6K-12K MRR. Reach via state food industry associations and food safety consultants who advise small distributors.
  • AI testimonial extractor from Gong calls (Peerbound space — single feature): Good bootstrap target for someone with B2B marketing experience. Don't build a full customer proof platform. Build ONE feature: "connect your Gong account, AI finds and extracts customer testimonials, posts them to a Slack channel daily." The buyer (customer marketing manager) evaluates on "I got 10 usable testimonials this week without asking anyone." $99-199/month per company × 50 companies = $5K-10K MRR. Reach via CMA (Customer Marketing Alliance) community, customer marketing LinkedIn groups, and the Slack-first distribution model. Integrate with Gong's API and G2 reviews to start.
  • AI cleaning quality verifier for STR turnovers (Trellis gap): Simple computer vision product for the STR market. Build an app where STR cleaners photograph each room after turnover. AI compares photos against a property-specific checklist (generated from the listing photos and amenity list). Flags missing items, unmade beds, dirty surfaces. Sends a "turnover verified" or "issues found" notification to the property manager. $3-5/month per property × 1,000 properties = $3K-5K MRR. Distribute via PMS app marketplaces (Guesty, Hostaway, Hostfully) and STR cleaning company partnerships. The cleaner-facing app has almost no competition.
  • AI apprenticeship compliance tracker for small solar installers (Dili space — gap): Regulation-driven niche for someone with construction/energy experience. The IRA's apprenticeship requirements are the most confusing part of prevailing wage compliance for small contractors. Build a standalone tool: input project details + workforce roster, track apprentice hours vs. journeyworker hours, flag when ratios fall below requirements, generate good-faith effort documentation. $99-199/month per contractor × 40 contractors = $4K-8K MRR. Reach via state solar energy associations (CALSEIA, TXSES) and the Solar Training Network.
