Gaps

  • AI for appraiser comparable selection and adjustment (software-only): Automax builds a full-service appraisal firm. But independent appraisers (78K in the US) still need a standalone tool that helps them pick and adjust comps. The current workflow: search MLS, pull 3-6 comps, manually adjust for differences (extra bathroom = +$X, no garage = -$Y). Nobody is building an AI tool that automates the adjustment calculation per comp based on local market data — the most time-consuming, judgment-heavy part of every appraisal. CoreLogic's tools are expensive and enterprise-focused.
  • AI for state-specific environmental compliance in construction: SchemeFlow covers major report types (traffic, noise, flood risk) for engineering firms. But smaller contractors doing residential or small commercial work still need SWPPP (Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plans), dust control plans, and NPDES permits. These are state- and county-specific, highly templated, and currently prepared by costly environmental consultants. Nobody is building "SchemeFlow for SWPPPs."
  • AI for insurance agency perpetual license management: Payna automates multi-state licensing for fintechs. But insurance agencies face a similar problem: each agent needs individual state licenses with different renewal dates, CE requirements, and appointment statuses across carriers. No AI-native tool exists for the insurance agency version of this problem — the adjacent market to both Payna and Kay.ai.
  • AI for CPA firm client document collection and organization: Magnetic automates data entry FROM documents INTO tax software. But the step before that — getting the documents from clients in the first place — is still done via email, portals (SafeSend, Canopy), and manual chasing. Nobody is building an AI agent that proactively communicates with tax clients to collect missing documents, verify completeness, and organize them before Magnetic-style processing begins.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI tax prep data entry for one tax software platform (Magnetic space): Excellent bootstrap target. Magnetic supports multiple tax software platforms. A bootstrapper could own one platform deeply — e.g., "the best AI data entry for Drake Tax users." Drake has ~50,000 users, mostly small firms. Build the deepest integration: handle Drake-specific form layouts, support Drake's import format natively, build a Drake community following. At $500-800/firm/season, 20 firms = $10K MRR during season. The thin-wrapper risk is mitigated by the platform-specific integration depth. Off-season revenue: bookkeeping document processing for the same firms.
  • AI comp selection tool for independent appraisers (Automax space gap): Strong bootstrap target. Build a standalone web app that takes a subject property address and returns ranked, adjusted comparable sales with rationale — no LiDAR, no mobile app, just data + AI. Integrate with public MLS data via Spark API or local MLS feeds. Price at $99-199/month per appraiser. Reach $10K MRR with 50-100 appraisers. The UAD 3.6 transition creates urgency — appraisers need tools that output data in the new format. Accessible via appraiser associations (AI, ASA) and the AppraisersBlogs community.
  • AI for SWPPP/stormwater reports (SchemeFlow space gap): Promising niche bootstrap target. Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plans are required for every construction project that disturbs more than 1 acre of land. Currently prepared by environmental consultants at $2K-10K each. A bootstrapper could build an AI tool that generates a site-specific SWPPP from a grading plan + site survey data, with state-specific BMP (Best Management Practice) selections auto-populated. The buyer is small GCs and civil engineering firms. $200-500/report or $500-1K/month subscription. Target one state first (California has the most complex requirements and the most construction activity).
  • AI client document collection for CPA firms (Magnetic-adjacent gap): Excellent adjacent bootstrap target with cross-sell to Magnetic's customers. Build an AI agent that emails/texts tax clients, asks for missing documents, answers questions about what's needed, and organizes everything into a structured folder per client. The buyer is the same CPA firm that buys Magnetic. At $200-400/firm/month (year-round), 25-50 firms = $10K MRR. This is a higher-frequency engagement than Magnetic's seasonal product — firms collect documents year-round for business clients. Canopy and Liscio are existing client portals but none are AI-native with proactive outreach.
  • AI for insurance agent license management (Payna-adjacent gap): Moderate bootstrap target, requires insurance domain knowledge. Insurance agencies with 10-100 agents spend significant admin time tracking individual agent licenses, appointments, and CE credits across states. Build a SaaS tool that monitors license statuses, sends renewal reminders, auto-files renewals, and tracks CE completion. The buyer is the agency principal or compliance manager. At $50-100/agent/month, an agency with 20 agents = $1K-2K/month. 10 agencies = $10K-20K MRR. Accessible via agency associations (PIA, Big I). The insider-access barrier is lower than Payna's fintech-focused sales motion.
