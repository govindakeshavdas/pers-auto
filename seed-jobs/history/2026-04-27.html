  • AI for construction permit applications & building department interactions: Bild AI mentions "permit applications" in their scope but their current focus is takeoffs/cost estimation. Structured AI does drawing QA. Nobody is specifically building AI that navigates the municipal building permit process — assembling the permit package, filling out jurisdiction-specific forms, tracking status with the building department, and managing resubmissions after plan check corrections. Every contractor and homeowner dreads this process. The buyer is GCs and permit expediting firms.
  • AI for veterinary billing/insurance (vs. front desk): Dodo handles vet clinic front desk (calls, records, Rx refills). Toothy handles dental insurance billing. But nobody is building "Toothy for veterinary" — AI that handles pet insurance verification, claims submission, and denial management. Pet insurance adoption is growing 20%+ annually and vet clinics are just starting to process insurance claims at scale. The workflows differ from human medical billing (different code sets, different payers) and Toothy's dental expertise doesn't transfer.
  • AI for subcontractor prequalification in construction: Bild AI and Structured AI handle drawings. Fleetline and Cartage (prev. runs) handle freight. But in construction, GCs spend enormous time prequalifying subcontractors — verifying insurance certificates, safety records (EMR), bonding capacity, and past project references. This is still done via email, spreadsheets, and manual phone calls. No AI-native player is solving this specific workflow, even though the adjacent players (Procore, BuildingConnected) have basic features.
  • AI for dental lab order management: Toothy AI automates the insurance side of dental. But dental practices also manage complex relationships with dental labs (crowns, implants, aligners) — ordering, tracking, quality-checking, and reconciling lab invoices. This is a parallel back-office workflow that nobody is automating. The buyer is the same dental office manager that Toothy sells to.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI for construction blueprint takeoffs (Bild AI space): Excellent bootstrap target with the right trade-specific focus. The construction estimating market is deeply fragmented by trade. Bild is starting with flooring, which means every other trade (electrical, plumbing, concrete, steel, roofing) is unowned. The most plausible wedge: pick one trade you know (or can learn from a trade partner), build the most accurate takeoff tool for that specific trade's blueprint conventions, and sell directly to subcontractors who bid on 5-20 jobs/month. Each sub pays $200-500/month. Reach $10K MRR with 25-50 subs. The technical depth (CV models for trade-specific symbols) protects against thin-wrapper commoditization.
  • AI for property accounting (IronLedger space): Strong bootstrap target, PMS-specific wedge. IronLedger integrates with all major PMS platforms. A bootstrapper could pick one smaller PMS (e.g., Buildium, which serves smaller property managers) and build the deepest possible integration — becoming the "IronLedger for Buildium users" that IronLedger's broad approach can't match in depth. The property management market has 300,000+ companies in the US. At $500/month, you need 20 customers for $10K MRR. The buyers (property managers) are accessible via PM associations, local REIA meetups, and PMS app marketplaces.
  • AI for dental RCM (Toothy AI space): Good bootstrap target, high domain barrier. 200,000+ dental practices in the US, each spending $13B+ annually on back-office tasks. The wedge: build for one PMS only (e.g., Open Dental, which is open-source and has the most accessible API). Toothy must support all PMS platforms; a bootstrapper owning one PMS deeply has a distribution advantage within that ecosystem. The dental community (state dental associations, dental conferences) is accessible. $500-1,000/month per practice means 10-20 practices for $10K MRR. Caveat: you need to understand dental billing codes (CDT), insurance verification workflows, and HIPAA compliance — steep learning curve without dental industry experience.
  • AI for vet clinic insurance billing (gap): Emerging opportunity, timing-dependent. Pet insurance is growing 20%+ annually but adoption is still under 5% of US pets. Vet clinics are only beginning to process insurance claims at volume. A bootstrapper could build "Toothy for vet" — AI that handles pet insurance verification, claims submission, and denial management. The technical challenge is moderate (pet insurance has fewer payers and simpler code sets than human medical insurance). The buyer (vet practice manager) is the same person Dodo already sells front-desk AI to, creating a natural cross-sell opportunity. But the market may be too small today — worth revisiting in 12-18 months as pet insurance adoption accelerates.
  • AI for construction permit expediting (gap): Excellent niche for a bootstrapper with construction experience. Every residential and commercial project requires building permits. The process involves assembling drawings, filling out jurisdiction-specific forms, submitting to the building department, and managing corrections. This is currently handled by specialized "permit expediting" firms or by GCs' admin staff. Nobody is building AI for this. The most plausible wedge: pick one jurisdiction (e.g., Los Angeles, which has notoriously complex permitting) and build an AI that knows LADBS forms, requirements, and common correction items. Sell to local GCs and permit expediters at $200-500/project. The data moat grows with each successful permit — you learn jurisdiction-specific patterns that competitors can't replicate without the same volume.
  • AI voice intake for non-PI law firms (Caseflood space): Viable adjacent bootstrap play. Caseflood dominates personal injury intake. But immigration law, family law, criminal defense, estate planning, and employment law all have different intake scripts, qualifying criteria, and emotional registers. A bootstrapper could build "Caseflood for immigration law" — AI that handles intake calls in English and Spanish, qualifies visa type and timeline, and books consultations. Immigration firms are concentrated, accessible via bar associations, and desperate for intake help. The voice AI technical bar is lower for immigration intake (structured questions, less emotional than PI) than for Caseflood's domain.
