  • AI for HOA management (Assembly HOA space): Moderate opportunity, operationally heavy. The vertically integrated model (manage HOAs + build software) is defensible but requires hiring community managers and handling physical operations. It's not a pure software play. Vantaca's acquisition of HOAi created a well-funded incumbent. The opportunity for a new entrant is in a different geography (Assembly is CA-only) or a different segment (self-managed HOAs vs. professionally managed). Worthwhile if you want to build an ops-heavy vertical SaaS; not worthwhile as a pure tech play.
  • Construction document intelligence API (gap): High opportunity, moderate difficulty. Every construction AI startup rebuilds the same document parsing pipeline. A horizontal API for structured blueprint/code data could serve 10+ customers (Bild AI, Permitify, Govstream.ai, UpCodes, general contractors). The technical challenge (parsing diverse blueprint formats + maintaining up-to-date building codes) is significant but tractable. The business model mirrors Plaid/Twilio — usage-based API. No incumbent is focused on this. Worthwhile — clear infrastructure gap, multiple funded potential customers, and a Plaid-like usage-based model.
  • AI agent for construction bidding strategy (gap): High opportunity, data-dependent. Sits above Bild AI's estimation output and adds strategic intelligence (bid/no-bid decision, margin optimization, competitor analysis). Requires access to historical bid data, which general contractors guard closely. The moat would be the aggregated bid-outcome dataset. Worthwhile if you can get 5-10 GCs to share historical bid data; not worthwhile without data access — the model is only as good as its training set.
