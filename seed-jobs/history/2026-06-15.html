  • "Built the tool they wished they had after experiencing the problem at their previous company" — the dominant founding pattern continues: Userlens founders built it after losing customers invisibly at Wudpecker (25K users, 1K customers). Hotwash founder built it after personally debriefing fire incidents. Amby founders built it after seeing EMS documentation failures at ALLM and OM1. Gaps

  • AI for fire department pre-incident planning (Hotwash adjacent): Hotwash captures lessons AFTER incidents. But BEFORE incidents, fire officers must create pre-incident plans for target hazards (large commercial buildings, schools, hospitals) — documenting access points, hydrant locations, hazmat storage, and building construction. Currently done with paper forms or basic PDF editors. Nobody is building "AI pre-plan generator for fire departments" that ingests building data (county assessor records, fire inspection reports) and auto-generates a target hazard pre-plan with GIS integration. The buyer is the same fire chief who buys Hotwash. Reachable via FDIC International and state fire marshal associations.
  • AI for veterinary inventory and pharmacy management (Scritch adjacent): Scritch handles front-desk operations. But behind the desk, vet practices manage drug inventory (controlled substances like ketamine require DEA logging), vaccines with expiration dates, and surgical supplies — still on spreadsheets or basic PMS features. Nobody is building "AI inventory manager for vet practices" that tracks controlled substance logs, predicts restock timing based on appointment volume, and flags expiring medications. The buyer is the same practice manager who uses Scritch.
  • AI for EMS mutual aid coordination (Amby Health/Hotwash adjacent): When a large incident occurs, fire/EMS agencies rely on mutual aid from neighboring departments. Coordinating which units respond, tracking apparatus availability, and managing cross-jurisdictional billing is done via radio, phone calls, and spreadsheets. Nobody is building "AI mutual aid coordinator" that monitors CAD (computer-aided dispatch) data across departments and auto-coordinates resource allocation. The buyer is the regional EMS council or county emergency management director.
  • AI for independent hotel revenue management (Lance adjacent, much narrower): Lance handles operations (calls, requests, scheduling). But independent hotels and B&Bs still price rooms manually or use basic channel managers. Nobody is building "AI revenue manager for independent hotels on Cloudbeds" — one PMS, dynamic pricing only. The buyer is the owner of a 15-50 room independent hotel who can't afford IDeaS or Duetto ($1K+/month enterprise tools). Reachable via Cloudbeds App Marketplace and AAHOA (Asian American Hotel Owners Association) events.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI after-action review for fire departments (Hotwash space — enter alongside or compete): Best bootstrap target in this run. Hotwash proves the concept with 11 paying departments and zero churn. A bootstrapper should build the same thing — or better, a complementary product. "AI debrief recorder + training drill generator for fire departments" — record the debrief, generate a structured after-action report, AND produce training scenarios based on the lessons. $200-400/month per department × 30 departments = $6K-12K MRR. Distribution: FDIC International (30K attendees), state fire chiefs' associations, and Firehouse.com forums. The fire service is a tight community — one happy chief tells ten others. The market is 27,000 departments and ZERO AI competitors.
  • AI EMS billing auditor for small fire-based EMS (Amby Health space — narrower): Excellent target for someone with EMS or fire service experience. Amby Health targets all EMS agencies. A bootstrapper should focus on fire department EMS specifically — departments that run ambulances as a side function and under-bill because their firefighters aren't billing experts. Build a tool that reviews PCRs (patient care reports), flags missed charges, and suggests correct billing codes — for fire departments on ESO or ImageTrend only. One RMS integration, one output (billing audit report). $300-600/month per department × 20 departments = $6K-12K MRR. Distribution: state fire chiefs' associations and IAFC EMS section.
  • AI after-hours phone agent for veterinary clinics (Scritch space — narrower): Strong target for someone who's worked in a vet practice. Scritch is building the full AI operating system. A bootstrapper should sell ONE thing: "AI answers your after-hours calls, triages emergencies, and texts you if it's urgent." No scheduling, no records, no refills — just the overnight answering. This is the highest-pain moment (pet owners calling at 2 AM with an emergency) and the simplest to build (voice → triage decision tree → text alert or emergency vet referral). $100-200/month per practice × 40 practices = $4K-8K MRR. Distribution: state VMA conferences and veterinary Facebook groups.
  • AI churn alert dashboard for early-stage SaaS (Userlens space — much narrower): PLG-native target for someone who's run CS at a SaaS company. Userlens targets Series A+ companies with CS teams. No QBR decks, no playbooks — just the alert. $49-99/month × 50 companies = $2.5K-5K MRR. Distribute via Indie Hackers, SaaS Twitter, and Product Hunt.
