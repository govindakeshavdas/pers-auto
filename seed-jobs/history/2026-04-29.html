Nobody is combining vision models for plan reading with distributor-specific product catalogs to generate takeoffs that auto-populate quotes in the distributor's ERP. This is the "Ventura for pre-sales" opportunity.
  • AI for interconnection queue monitoring and strategy: Grid8 helps developers submit applications. But once you're in queue, the 3-5 year wait involves constant monitoring: study results, cluster studies, cost allocation changes, and withdrawal decisions by neighboring projects that affect your position. Nobody is building an AI that monitors queue movements, predicts timeline changes, and recommends strategic actions (withdraw, upgrade, hold) based on real-time queue data from OASIS/PJM/MISO portals.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI quoting/order entry for one distribution sub-vertical (Ventura/Distro space): Best bootstrap target in this entire report series. The pattern is proven: Distro (HVAC), Ventura (building materials), Burnt (food), Smartbase (manufacturing) — all doing the same thing for different verticals. Dozens of sub-verticals remain: electrical distribution, plumbing supply, bearings/MRO, janitorial supply, auto parts, industrial gases, packaging materials. Pick one. Build the deepest integration for one ERP (e.g., Eclipse for electrical, P21 for plumbing). A bootstrapper with 2-3 years of experience at a distributor would have unfair advantage. Charge $500-1K/seat/month. 10-15 seats at 2-3 branches = $10K MRR.
  • AI ops agent for CPG brands on one e-commerce/ERP platform (Corvera space): Excellent bootstrap target. Corvera targets brands selling into retail (Shopify → retailer). A bootstrapper could focus on a different channel: "AI ops agent for Amazon FBA sellers" or "AI inventory management for Faire wholesale brands." The CPG brand market is so fragmented that platform-specific specialization is a viable wedge. $1K-2K/month per brand, 8 brands = $10K MRR. The Expo West / Natural Products Expo community is accessible for customer development.
  • AI receptionist for medspas (Tepali space — narrower wedge): Strong bootstrap target. Don't build the full OS. Build the best AI phone answering + booking agent specifically for medspas. Train it on treatment menus, pricing, provider availability, and common patient questions ("How long does Botox last?" "Can I combine filler with laser?"). Sell as a standalone product or plugin to existing PMS platforms (Zenoti, Jane App). $200-500/month/location. 30 locations = $6K-15K MRR. The AmSpa community and medspa Facebook groups are direct distribution channels.
  • AI for FCC certification documentation (Noetic space — narrower wedge): Moderate bootstrap target, niche but viable. FCC Part 15 (consumer electronics, IoT devices) is the most common certification path. Build an AI tool that takes a product's technical specs and generates FCC pre-compliance documentation, test plans, and lab-ready files. Charge $2K-5K per product certification cycle. The buyer is hardware startups (accessible via Hacker News, Product Hunt, hardware accelerators). 3-5 certifications per month = $10K+ MRR. The risk: LLMs may not be accurate enough for this without significant fine-tuning on FCC standards.
  • AI for equipment rental dynamic pricing (Chasi space — gap): Promising niche, requires equipment rental domain knowledge. Build a pricing optimization tool that ingests a dealer's rental fleet data, local competitor pricing (scraped from BigRentz, United Rentals), and demand signals (construction permits, weather) to recommend optimal rental rates. The buyer is the rental manager at a mid-size equipment dealer. Charge per-asset/month ($5-10/asset × 200 assets = $1K-2K/month per dealer). 10 dealers = $10K-20K MRR. This is purely software — no hardware component — and solves a problem adjacent to but distinct from Chasi's request-handling.
