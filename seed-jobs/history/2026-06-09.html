But after the invoice is paid, CPG brands face a nightmare of retailer deductions — chargebacks for short shipments, late deliveries, incorrect labeling, and pricing discrepancies. These deductions can consume 2-5% of gross revenue and are managed in spreadsheets. Nobody is building "AI deduction manager for CPG brands" that ingests retailer deduction reports (Walmart, Target, Kroger each have different formats), classifies the deduction type, validates against shipping records, and auto-generates dispute claims. The buyer is the same ops person who buys Jampack. Reachable via Startup CPG Slack and CPG-specific finance communities.
  • AI for factoring company fraud detection (Zolvo adjacent): Zolvo automates payment matching and invoice verification. But the step before funding — detecting fraudulent invoices (duplicate invoices, fictitious receivables, collusion between borrowers and their customers) — is still done by manual audit. Nobody is building "AI fraud screener for factoring companies" that cross-references invoice details against public records, shipping data, and historical patterns to flag suspicious submissions before funding. The buyer is the risk manager at the same factoring company that uses Zolvo.
  • AI for cemetery genealogy/family search (Memora adjacent): Memora manages cemetery operations for administrators. But families searching for ancestors' graves still rely on FindAGrave (volunteer-maintained, often incomplete) or calling cemetery offices. Nobody is building "AI cemetery search for families" that takes historical burial records (often handwritten ledgers, PDF scans), digitizes them with OCR + AI, and makes them searchable. The buyer is the same cemetery administrator who uses Memora, but the value extends to families and genealogy researchers (Ancestry.com, FamilySearch users).
  • AI for small clinic prior authorization (DrSwarm adjacent): DrSwarm handles scheduling, billing, and RCM. But the most painful step in healthcare admin — prior authorization (getting insurer approval before procedures) — requires phone calls, fax submissions, and 3-14 day waits. Nobody is building a standalone "AI prior auth submission tool for independent practices" that auto-generates the required clinical documentation from the EHR, submits via ePA portals, and tracks status. The buyer is the same practice manager who buys DrSwarm. This is specifically the prior auth step, not full RCM — narrow enough to bootstrap.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI wholesale order processor for one CPG sub-category (Jampack space — narrower): Best bootstrap target in this run. Jampack covers the full order-to-cash lifecycle for all CPG brands. A bootstrapper should pick ONE segment: "AI wholesale PO processor for craft beverage brands on QuickBooks" or "AI freight rate finder for snack brands selling into Whole Foods." The buyer (ops founder at a $2M-$10M brand) decides in one demo — "does this save me 3 hours this week?" Build the simplest version: email forwarding rule that captures POs, AI parses them, creates draft orders in QuickBooks, and sends a confirmation. $200-400/month per brand × 30 brands = $6K-12K MRR. Distribute via Startup CPG Slack (10K+ members), BevNET community, and Expo West/Fancy Food Show.
  • AI invoice verifier for small factoring companies (Zolvo space — narrower): Excellent bootstrap target for someone with commercial lending experience. Zolvo covers the full servicing stack. A bootstrapper should build just the invoice verification step: "AI that validates invoices against purchase orders and shipping records for factoring companies using FactorSoft." Upload invoices → AI extracts key fields → matches against PO data → flags discrepancies → generates verification report. $300-800/month per lender × 15 lenders = $4.5K-12K MRR. Distribute via International Factoring Association (IFA, 300+ member companies) and Secured Finance Network.
  • AI cemetery map digitizer as a services-to-SaaS play (Memora space — narrower): Unique niche for someone comfortable with GIS tools. Memora's full platform includes operations, family portals, and field team apps. A bootstrapper should sell ONE thing: "I will digitize your paper cemetery map into an interactive, searchable digital map." Charge $1K-3K per map conversion (PDF → geo-referenced GIS). Target the ~22K active US cemeteries, most of which have never digitized their maps. After converting 20+ cemeteries, build a self-service platform and switch to monthly SaaS ($100-200/month per cemetery for hosting and updates). Year 1: 5 maps/month × $2K = $10K/month in services. Year 2: convert services customers to $150/month SaaS × 60 cemeteries = $9K MRR. Distribute via state cemetery associations and ICCFA.
  • AI deduction manager for DTC-to-wholesale CPG brands (Jampack gap): High-WTP niche for someone with CPG finance experience. Retailer deductions consume 2-5% of gross revenue — a $10M brand loses $200K-$500K/year. Build a tool that ingests retailer deduction reports (Walmart Retail Link, Target Partners Online, Kroger 84.51°), classifies the deduction type (pricing, logistics, compliance), and auto-generates dispute claims. $500-1K/month per brand × 20 brands = $10K-20K MRR. The ROI is immediate — if you recover even 20% of deductions, the tool pays for itself in week one.
  • AI prior auth submission for independent practices on one EHR (DrSwarm gap): Strong niche for someone with healthcare admin experience. Prior authorization is the most hated administrative task in healthcare — requires an average of 45 minutes per request. Build a tool that integrates with one EHR (eClinicalWorks, AdvancedMD), pulls the relevant clinical documentation, auto-generates the prior auth submission, and files via the insurer's ePA portal. $200-400/month per practice × 25 practices = $5K-10K MRR. Distribute via medical specialty associations (dermatology, orthopedics — specialties with the highest prior auth volume).
