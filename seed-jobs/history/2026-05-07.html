Every regulated back-office function follows the same automation sequence: ingest unstructured inputs → validate against rules → generate structured output → post to system of record → produce audit trail. The vertical determines the rules and SOR.
  • AI for consulting displacement — "$500/hr consultant replaced by AI" framing: Huscarl (actuarial advisory), Fed10 (legislative consulting), DiligenceSquared (McKinsey-style PE due diligence from YC listing), Stilta (patent research — Run 8). The pattern: high-WTP professional services where the deliverable is a structured analysis document. AI generates the first draft; human expert reviews. The consulting displacement framing creates immediate ROI proof.

Gaps

  • AI for residential contractor financing/payment collection: Scout Out and Handoff both handle invoicing but neither solves the core cash-flow problem: contractors wait 30-90 days for payment while fronting material costs. Nobody is building "AI collections agent for residential contractors" — an agent that sends payment reminders, escalates overdue invoices, and optionally offers instant payment financing. This is the natural next layer above Scout Out/Handoff's invoice feature, where the pain (cash-flow crunches kill more contractors than bad work) is acute and the buyer is already in the estimation tool.
  • AI for mining exploration targeting (the step before Deep Core's modelling): Deep Core helps geologists model what's underground. But the step before — deciding WHERE to explore in the first place — is still done with desktop GIS tools and intuition. Nobody is building "AI site selection for mineral exploration" that synthesizes satellite data, geochemistry surveys, historical drill results, and geological maps to recommend optimal drill locations. The buyer (VP Exploration at junior miners) would pay $10K+/month for a tool that reduces the number of barren holes drilled.
  • AI for insurance renewal benchmarking (the SMB version of Huscarl): Huscarl targets $50M+ companies. But the ~6M small businesses in the US that renew commercial insurance annually face the same problem: they don't know if their premiums are fair because they have no comparison data. Nobody is building "AI insurance renewal audit for SMBs" — a tool that a small business owner feeds their renewal quote into and gets a benchmarked analysis showing whether they're over/under-paying relative to similar businesses. Sell to the business owner, not the broker. $50-100/audit × thousands of renewals = meaningful revenue.
  • AI for state-specific regulatory compliance calendars (the SMB version of Fed10): Fed10 monitors bills for large government affairs teams. But every small business in regulated industries (cannabis, liquor, childcare, healthcare) faces recurring state compliance deadlines (license renewals, reporting requirements, tax filings) that they track on spreadsheets or miss entirely. Nobody is building "AI compliance calendar for [state] [industry] operators" — a tool that ingests a business's licenses and automatically tracks every deadline, generates required filings, and sends reminders. The buyer is the SMB owner, not the government affairs team.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI estimating for one residential trade in one metro (Scout Out/Handoff space — narrower): Best bootstrap target in this run. Don't compete with Scout Out on "all residential." Pick ONE trade (roofing, painting, HVAC, plumbing) and ONE metro (Dallas, Phoenix, Atlanta). Build the most accurate AI estimating tool for that trade using local labor rates and local supplier pricing. Residential contractors in that trade will find you via Google ("roofing estimate software Dallas"), trade-specific Facebook groups, and local Home Builders Association meetings. $99-149/month × 50 contractors = $5K-7.5K MRR. Then expand to adjacent metros or trades.
  • AI for insurance billing calls at independent billing companies (LunaBill space — narrower buyer): Strong bootstrap target for someone with healthcare billing experience. LunaBill targets hospital systems (enterprise). A bootstrapper should target the ~100K independent medical billing companies (many 1-5 person operations) that outsource billing for small practices. These buyers evaluate on "how many more claims can my 2-person team follow up per day" — no enterprise procurement cycle. Build an AI caller that handles the three most common payer calls (claim status, prior auth status, appeal follow-up) for the three most common billing platforms (Kareo/Tebra, AdvancedMD, athenaCollector). $300-800/month per billing company × 15 = $4.5K-12K MRR.
  • AI for geologic modelling in ONE sub-sector (Deep Core space — narrower): Niche bootstrap target requiring geology expertise. Deep Core targets all mineral exploration. A bootstrapper should target ONE application: "AI for geothermal well siting" (growing fast due to IRA incentives), "AI for carbon storage site characterization" (regulatory demand), or "AI for groundwater modelling for civil engineering." Each sub-sector has its own conferences (GRC for geothermal, AAPG for carbon storage), its own data formats, and its own buyer persona. At $2K-5K/month per project × 5 active projects = $10K-25K MRR.
  • AI insurance renewal benchmarking for SMBs (Huscarl space — gap): Promising gap with a simple wedge. Build a tool where a small business owner uploads their commercial insurance renewal quote. AI parses the policy, compares premiums to benchmarks (public rate filings, NAIC data), and produces a one-page report showing whether they're overpaying. Charge $49-99 per audit or $199/year for ongoing monitoring. The buyer is the SMB owner or their bookkeeper — reachable via accountant referrals, Yelp ads, and small business communities (SCORE, local chambers of commerce). 100 audits/month × $49 = $4.9K MRR. The benchmarking data comes from publicly filed rate schedules, not proprietary sources.
  • AI compliance calendar for one state + one regulated industry (Fed10 space — gap): Narrow but viable for a regulatory-savvy bootstrapper. Build "compliance calendar for California cannabis operators" or "license tracking for Texas childcare providers." Each regulated industry in each state has 10-30 recurring deadlines per year. The tool ingests the business's license numbers, auto-generates filing documents, and sends deadline reminders. Sell at $99-199/month per business. In California alone there are ~7,000 licensed cannabis businesses. 40 customers × $149/month = $6K MRR. Expand state by state.
