The shared architecture: ingest regulatory corpus → match content/application against rules → flag violations → route for approval. Each vertical has its own regulatory body (FINRA, IRB, CMS, OSHA) but the underlying AI pattern is identical. The race is to build the deepest regulatory knowledge base per vertical.
  • "Caregiver/staff coordination" emerging as a multi-vertical AI cluster with overlapping buyers: Vali Health (home care agency caregiver shift-fill), Norra (nursing facility equipment tracking, Run 17), Netter (nursing home invoice collection, Run 17). The same buyer — facility administrator or agency owner in senior care — manages scheduling, equipment, billing, and compliance across disconnected systems. The coordination problem is identical to property management (CentralComs, Run 17) but for care rather than buildings.
  • "Solo non-technical founder hiring first engineer" as the clearest bootstrap-adjacent signal across all runs: Quin (13 yrs private banking, no technical background, hiring founding engineer/CTO), Hotwash (West Point/Yale Law founder, built entire product himself, Run 18), Warrant CEO Austin Carroll (fintech marketer, not an engineer). These founders are domain experts who saw the problem but lack the technical co-founder — exactly the pattern where an engineer could bootstrap a competitor by combining coding ability with the same domain insight.
  • "AI that replaces the 20-25% professional services fee" — the recurring bootstrappable disruption pattern: Superposition (replaces recruiter's 20-25% fee), Warrant (replaces compliance consultant's hourly fees), LabOps (replaces IRB consulting fees). The shared insight: professional services with a formulaic process but high fees are the best targets for AI automation. The fee structure creates an obvious "10x cheaper" value prop for the AI tool.

Gaps

  • AI for home care EVV (Electronic Visit Verification) compliance (Vali Health adjacent): Vali automates scheduling and shift-fill. But since the 21st Century Cures Act mandated EVV for Medicaid-funded home care, every agency must prove caregivers arrived on time, stayed the required duration, and performed the correct services. EVV compliance is managed through clunky state-mandated systems (Sandata, HHAeXchange) with frequent errors. Nobody is building "AI EVV compliance auditor for home care agencies" that automatically verifies visit records, flags discrepancies before state audit, and auto-corrects common errors. The buyer is the same agency owner who buys Vali.
  • AI for small fleet repair invoice auditing (Carma adjacent, much smaller): Carma targets Fortune 500 fleets with 1,000+ vehicles. But owner-operators and small fleets (5-50 trucks) get overcharged on repairs constantly — they don't have a fleet manager reviewing every invoice. Nobody is building "AI repair invoice auditor for small trucking companies" that photographs the repair invoice, cross-references with standard labor times (Mitchell, AllData), and flags overcharges. The buyer is the independent trucking company owner who manages their own vehicles.
  • AI for conference/IRB pre-submission check (LabOps adjacent, narrower): LabOps automates the full IRB workflow. But the specific pain for graduate students and postdocs is the pre-submission step: "Is my protocol application complete? Will it get sent back for missing fields?" Nobody is building a standalone "AI protocol completeness checker" that reads the draft IRB application and highlights missing sections, inconsistent dates, and incomplete consent language BEFORE the researcher submits. The buyer is the individual graduate student or postdoc, not the institution.
  • AI for event promoter settlement reconciliation (Hive adjacent): Hive helps promoters market their events. But after the event, the settlement process (reconciling ticket sales, fees, artist guarantees, venue expenses, tax deductions) is done in spreadsheets. Nobody is building "AI settlement reconciliation for independent concert promoters" that ingests Ticketmaster/Eventbrite sales data, venue contracts, and artist rider/guarantee terms to auto-generate the settlement statement. The buyer is the independent promoter or venue manager who currently does this by hand for every show.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI marketing compliance checker for one regulated industry (Warrant space — narrower): Best bootstrap target in this run for someone with fintech marketing experience. Warrant covers 1,500+ rules across all regulated industries. A bootstrapper should pick ONE: "AI ad copy compliance checker for independent insurance agencies in [one state]." The state DOI (Department of Insurance) has specific advertising rules — build a tool that checks an insurance agent's social media post or email against those rules and provides fix suggestions. $49-99/month per agency × 40 agencies = $2K-4K MRR. Distribute via state insurance agent associations (each state has one) and Insurance Marketing & Communications Association (IMCA). The key: state-level specificity is a defensible wedge that a national platform like Warrant won't prioritize.
  • AI shift-fill texting agent for home care agencies (Vali Health space — narrower): Excellent target for someone with home care operations experience. Vali automates the full agency backend. A bootstrapper should sell ONE thing: "AI that fills your empty shifts by texting available caregivers." When a caregiver calls out, the tool texts all qualified available caregivers, collects responses, and confirms the fill — in 10 minutes, not the 2 hours it takes the office manager to make 15 phone calls. $100-200/month per agency × 30 agencies = $3K-6K MRR. Distribute via state home care associations and Caring.com industry forums. Build on top of the agency's existing scheduling system (ClearCare, AxisCare).
  • AI IRB protocol drafter for graduate students (LabOps space — narrower, individual buyer): PLG-native target for someone who's been through the IRB process. LabOps targets institutions. A bootstrapper should go direct to researchers: "AI tool that drafts your IRB protocol application from your study description." Input: 500-word description of your study. Output: complete draft IRB application with all required sections pre-filled, consent language templates, and a completeness checklist. $15-30/month per researcher or $50-100 per application. Market to graduate programs directly via department listservs and Reddit r/GradSchool (500K+ subscribers). 200 researchers × $25/month = $5K MRR. No institutional procurement needed.
  • AI repair invoice auditor for small trucking companies (Carma gap): Strong niche for someone with fleet maintenance experience. Owner-operators and small fleets (5-50 trucks) spend $2K-5K/month per vehicle on maintenance and repairs. Build a tool that photographs the repair invoice, OCRs the line items, cross-references against standard labor times (Mitchell, AllData databases), and flags overcharges. "This shop charged 4 hours for a brake job that should take 2.5." $50-100/month per fleet × 50 fleets = $2.5K-5K MRR. Distribute via OOIDA (Owner-Operator Independent Drivers Association, 150K+ members) and trucking-specific subreddits.
  • AI event settlement reconciliation for independent promoters (Hive gap): Niche target for someone with live music/event experience. After every concert, the promoter must reconcile ticket sales, venue fees, artist guarantees, and expenses into a settlement. Currently done in Excel. Build a tool that ingests Ticketmaster/Eventbrite CSV exports, venue contracts, and artist deals → auto-generates the settlement statement. $50-100/month per promoter or $25 per settlement × 200 events/month = $5K MRR. Distribute via Pollstar events, NIVA (National Independent Venue Association), and promoter Facebook groups.
