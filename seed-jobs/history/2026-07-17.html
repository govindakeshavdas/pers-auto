All three explicitly describe themselves as profitable and bootstrapped (or bootstrapped-to-revenue-before-raising). The shared insight: vertical SaaS companies that ship a great product can win on product merit in markets where VC-funded competitors are burning cash on sales and marketing. The verticals where this works best: logistics, web data, developer tools — markets where the buyer evaluates product quality directly.
  • "AI for home services" has exploded into a 4-company VC cluster in July 2026: Avoca ($1B, inbound calls), Probook ($40M, dispatch, this run), Faraday (back-office paperwork, this run), and the broader ServiceTitan ($9B+) ecosystem. The home services vertical ($600B) now has more VC funding flowing into AI than any other vertical identified across all runs. Signal: the opportunity was real 12 months ago, now it's a red ocean for bootstrappers at the "full OS" level — but specific workflows (permits, warranties, rebates per Faraday) remain open.
  • "GEO / AI search visibility" emerged as a new bootstrappable category this run: Evertune ($19M, enterprise), SEORCE (bootstrapped, SMB), Peec AI (bootstrapped, $99/month), and 50+ other tools. The market is moving from ~$15M in 2023 to a projected $280M in 2026. The 60× price gap between Evertune ($3K/month) and SMB tools ($49/month) represents a massive middle market. Every DTC brand, SaaS company, and B2B startup needs to understand how they appear in ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini answers.
  • "AI plugs into your existing systems" — the 'dark software' pattern is now the dominant GTM across all runs: Orbit (sits on top of logistics ERPs), Nomerra (plugs into existing ERP/banking/docs), Whitespace (Run 27, sits on distributor ERPs), MarkIt (Run 27, inside Excel/broker portals), Denki (Run 29, works on AuditBoard/Workiva). The pattern: don't replace the system of record, automate the workflows around it.

Gaps

  • AI IRA rebate/permit filer for individual HVAC contractors (Faraday gap, one workflow): Faraday automates the full home services back-office. But 100,000+ independent HVAC contractors installing heat pumps and energy-efficient systems need to file IRA energy efficiency rebate applications — each worth $2K–$8K per unit — and most don't know the forms exist or how to file them. Nobody is building a standalone "upload your installation invoice + equipment model number → AI auto-fills the utility rebate application + IRA 25C tax credit form + manufacturer warranty registration." The buyer is the independent HVAC installer. Distribute via ACCA (Air Conditioning Contractors of America, 60,000+ members) and HVAC supply houses. $29–79/month per contractor.
  • AI email-to-order converter for small freight forwarders (Orbit gap, one workflow): Orbit builds the full logistics OS. But 50,000+ small freight forwarders (5–20 employees) receive 100+ order emails per day and manually re-key them into their TMS. Nobody is building a standalone "forward your order emails → AI extracts pickup/delivery address, dimensions, weight, commodity → creates structured shipment in your existing TMS." The buyer is the operations clerk at a small freight broker. Distribute via TIA (Transportation Intermediaries Association, 8,000+ members). $100–300/month per broker.
  • AI concept renderer as a SketchUp plugin for small architecture firms (xFigura gap, different tool): xFigura targets the top 20 firms with Rhino/Revit workflows. But 100,000+ small architecture firms (2–10 people) use SketchUp, not Rhino or Revit, and can't afford xFigura's enterprise pricing. Nobody is building "SketchUp plugin → select a view → AI generates a photorealistic concept render in your style → export for client presentation." The buyer is the sole practitioner or small-firm principal architect. Distribute via SketchUp Extension Warehouse and architecture Instagram. $19–49/month per user.
  • Free AI brand visibility checker — "is your brand visible in ChatGPT?" (Evertune/SEORCE gap, freemium wedge): Evertune charges $3K/month. SEORCE and Peec AI charge $49–99/month. But 5M+ small businesses have no idea whether ChatGPT recommends them or their competitors. Nobody is building a truly free "enter your brand name + category → we check 5 AI platforms → you get a free visibility score + top competitor comparison." The buyer is the small business owner or indie SaaS founder. Distribute virally via "check your AI visibility" Twitter/LinkedIn posts. Free → $29/month premium × 40 users = $1.2K MRR.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI email-to-shipment converter for small freight brokers (Orbit space — one workflow): Best bootstrap target in this run for someone with logistics experience. Orbit proves the logistics OS is bootstrappable to profitability. A bootstrapper should build ONE tool for ONE workflow: email → structured shipment. Build a tool: (1) forward your order emails to orders@[yourtool].com, (2) AI extracts sender, pickup address, delivery address, dimensions, weight, commodity, requested dates, (3) creates a structured shipment record you can copy into any TMS, (4) flags exceptions for human review. The freight broker currently copies this manually 100+ times/day. $100–300/month per broker × 25 brokers = $2.5K–7.5K MRR. Distribute via TIA events (8,000+ members) and freight broker Facebook groups.
  • Free AI brand visibility checker + premium upgrade (Evertune/SEORCE space — freemium): Highest viral potential, fastest to build. Evertune and SEORCE prove the GEO market is real. A bootstrapper should build the freemium entry point the enterprise players refuse to offer. Build a web tool: (1) enter your brand name + category (e.g., "Notion, project management"), (2) tool queries ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini with 5 standard prompts, (3) shows you a free "AI Visibility Score" — how often you're mentioned, sentiment, top competitors, (4) premium tier unlocks daily monitoring, historical trends, and actionable recommendations. Free tier drives viral sharing ("I'm a 23/100 on AI visibility!"). $29–79/month premium × 40 users = $1.2K–3.2K MRR. Distribute via "check your AI visibility" posts on Twitter/LinkedIn that founders will reshare.
  • AI concept renderer SketchUp plugin for small architecture firms (xFigura space — different tool): Best target for someone with architecture/design experience. xFigura proves architecture firms will pay for AI design tools ($0→$1M ARR). A bootstrapper should target the 100K+ small firms on SketchUp (not the top 20 firms on Rhino/Revit). Build a SketchUp extension: (1) select a 3D view in SketchUp, (2) choose a render style (photorealistic, watercolor, line-drawing), (3) AI generates a high-quality concept image in 30 seconds, (4) export at presentation resolution. $19–49/month per user × 40 users = $760–$1,960 MRR. Distribute via SketchUp Extension Warehouse (zero CAC) and architecture YouTube tutorials.
  • AI IRA rebate filer for HVAC contractors (Faraday space — one form): Strongest regulatory tailwind, simplest to build. Faraday proves home services back-office AI works (300% growth). A bootstrapper should build ONE tool: IRA rebate application filing. Build a web tool: (1) upload your installation invoice + equipment model number, (2) AI identifies applicable rebates (utility company rebate, IRA 25C federal tax credit, manufacturer warranty), (3) auto-fills each application form, (4) provides filing instructions and deadlines. The HVAC contractor currently doesn't file because they don't know what forms exist. $29–79/month per contractor × 30 contractors = $870–$2,370 MRR. Distribute via ACCA chapters (60K members), HVAC supply house bulletin boards, and HVAC YouTube channels. The pitch: "you're leaving $2K–$8K per install on the table because you don't file the rebate."
  • AI take-home test scorer for startup hiring managers (Comograph space — one feature): Fastest to build, lowest barrier to adoption. Comograph proves that proof-based hiring resonates. A bootstrapper should build ONE tool: score existing take-home submissions. Build a web tool: (1) paste your take-home assignment prompt, (2) upload the candidate's submission (code repo or zip), (3) AI analyzes code quality, architecture decisions, documentation, test coverage, and communication clarity, (4) generates a structured score with specific callouts. The engineering manager currently spends 2–4 hours reviewing each submission manually. $29–79/month per active search × 20 searches = $580–$1,580 MRR. Distribute via engineering manager LinkedIn groups and HN "Who's Hiring" threads.
