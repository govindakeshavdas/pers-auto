  • AI for manufacturing operations — now a 6+ company cluster across YC batches alone: Korso (RFQ/quote/supplier coordination), Arzana (quoting/order entry — Run 12), Comena (distributor order processing — Run 12), Smartbase (PO entry — Run 13), Poka Labs (chemical manufacturing ops — Run 12), Pairio (maintenance knowledge — Run 12), Sidekick (frontline worker SMS assistant). Each targets a different layer of the manufacturing operations stack, but the shared architecture is identical: parse unstructured input → match to business logic → execute in ERP → confirm. The sheer density of startups here signals that manufacturing operations is the most underdigitized white-collar workflow in B2B.
  • "Domain expert who ran the exact business" as the dominant founding pattern — now 8+ examples: Corvera CEO ran Better Nature (CPG brand, 5,000 stores). Bravi CEO grew up in family shutter installation business. Sidekick founder's father owns a manufacturing facility. Dayjob founders spent mornings in waste depots (Run 13). Patientdesk founders ran a dental call center (Run 11). Cohesive founders ran a $50K MRR service business (Run 11). These companies consistently show faster traction than pure-tech founders entering a new vertical. The pattern is now undeniable.
  • AI for fragmented "trades" businesses — expanding into ultra-niche verticals: Bravi (fenestration: shutters, windows, garage doors), Cohesive (janitorial, landscaping — Run 11), Ressl (general trades — Run 8), Acrely (home services — Run 8), Drillbit (residential contractors). Each targets a different trade vertical with the same front-office automation architecture (missed calls → AI → lead capture → booking). The fenestration niche is the most specific yet — signals that viable niches exist in even smaller trade sub-categories.
  • CPG brand operations as a new AI sub-ecosystem: Corvera (order processing, inventory, demand forecasting for retail brands), Comena (distributor order processing — Run 12). CPG brands have unique operational pain: small teams (5-15 people), selling into major retailers who demand EDI compliance, managing inventory across multiple warehouses/channels. This is the same "glue work" pattern seen in manufacturing, but for the consumer goods supply chain.

Gaps

  • AI for trade-specific product configurators (Bravi adjacent): Bravi captures fenestration leads and qualifies them. But the step after qualification — configuring the right product (shutter size, material, color, motorization, mounting type) — is still done manually by the installer during a site visit or over lengthy phone calls. Nobody is building "AI window/shutter configurator for installers" where the homeowner answers 5-10 questions via chat (room dimensions, window photos, style preference) and gets an instant preliminary quote. The buyer is the same installer who uses Bravi for lead capture. This exists for solar (Aurora Solar) but not for fenestration, garage doors, or custom cabinetry.
  • AI for CPG retailer compliance (Corvera adjacent): Corvera handles orders, inventory, and forecasting. But CPG brands selling into major retailers (Walmart, Target, Whole Foods) face a nightmare of retailer-specific compliance requirements: EDI formatting, labeling specs, packaging dimensions, routing guides, chargebacks. Nobody is building "AI retailer compliance checker for CPG brands" that takes a product listing and automatically validates it against each retailer's specific requirements (GDSN data pool submissions, SCS/SDS filings, COOL labeling). The buyer is the same ops person at the CPG brand who buys Corvera.
  • AI for small CPA firm client intake/organizer (Marble adjacent): Marble does tax research and memo drafting for CPAs. But the step before research — collecting client documents (W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, receipts) — is still done via email chains, client portals that nobody uses, or physical folders. Nobody is building "AI tax document collector for solo CPAs" where the client texts or emails their documents, the AI classifies them (W-2, 1099-INT, 1099-NEC, etc.), extracts key fields, and organizes them into a tax-ready package. The buyer is the solo CPA who prepares 200-500 returns per season. SafeSend and Canopy exist but are not AI-native.
  • AI for commodity trade document verification/matching (CommodityAI adjacent, narrower): CommodityAI processes and classifies commodity documents. But the specific pain of three-way matching (contract vs. bill of lading vs. invoice) for commodity shipments is still done manually by operations teams. Nobody is building a standalone "AI three-way matcher for commodity trades" that takes a trade confirmation, matches it against the B/L and commercial invoice, and flags discrepancies (weight variance, quality specification mismatches, price differences). The buyer is the same trading ops team CommodityAI serves.

Bootstrap Viability

  • AI SMS helpdesk for one type of shop floor (Sidekick space — narrower): Best bootstrap target in this run. Sidekick is going horizontal across manufacturing, auto dealerships, and retail. A bootstrapper should pick ONE facility type with a common knowledge problem: "AI text assistant for auto body shops" (paint codes, repair procedures, parts lookups) or "AI SMS helpdesk for print shops" (press settings, color matching, paper specs). The SMS delivery means zero app store approval, zero IT involvement, zero training. Upload SOPs, get a phone number, workers text. $99-149/month per shop × 40 shops = $4K-6K MRR. Reach via trade-specific Facebook groups (Auto Body Professionals — 50K+ members, PrintingUnited community). The compounding knowledge base is the real moat — every answer captured makes the system more valuable for that specific shop.
  • AI lead capture for one fenestration sub-vertical (Bravi space — narrower): Excellent bootstrap target for someone in the trades. Bravi started with shutters in France and is expanding to adjacent trades. A bootstrapper should claim ONE ultra-niche US trade: "AI answering service for custom garage door installers" or "AI lead qualifier for residential window replacement companies." These installers get 10-30 inbound calls/day and miss 30-40% of them. Build a simple voice AI that answers missed calls, qualifies the lead (project type, timeline, budget), and texts the installer a summary. $200-400/month per installer × 25 installers = $5K-10K MRR. Reach via Angi/HomeAdvisor installer networks, trade show booths at GlassBuild America (fenestration), IDA Expo (garage doors).
  • AI order processing for DTC CPG brands on Shopify (Corvera space — narrower): Good bootstrap target for someone with e-commerce/CPG experience. Corvera serves brands with $50M+ ARR selling into retail. A bootstrapper should go smaller: "AI wholesale order processor for Shopify-based food brands" — parse wholesale PO emails, create orders in Shopify, generate invoices, track shipments. The buyer is the founder/ops person at a food/beverage brand doing $500K-$5M/year who processes wholesale orders in spreadsheets. $150-300/month per brand × 30 brands = $4.5K-9K MRR. Reach via Startup CPG Slack community (10K+ members), Specialty Food Association events, and DTC brand Facebook groups.
  • AI tax document collector for solo CPAs (Marble space — gap): Strong seasonal bootstrap target for someone with tax preparation experience. Build a tool where a solo CPA's clients text or email their tax documents (W-2 photos, 1099 PDFs, receipt scans). The AI classifies each document, extracts key fields (employer name, wages, withholding), and organizes everything into a client folder ready for preparation. Charge per-client during tax season: $3-5/client × 300 clients × 50 CPAs = $45K-75K in seasonal revenue, or $99-149/month year-round × 50 CPAs = $5K-7.5K MRR. Reach via state CPA society listservs and the Accounting Today/CPA Practice Advisor community.
  • AI product configurator for custom home improvement quotes (Bravi gap): Niche but high-WTP for someone with fenestration or custom home improvement experience. Build a chatbot where a homeowner inputs room dimensions, takes a photo of their window/door, and gets an instant preliminary quote for shutters/blinds/windows. The installer reviews and adjusts. No site visit needed for the initial estimate. $100-250/month per installer × 30 installers = $3K-7.5K MRR. Reach via the same fenestration installer communities Bravi targets. This complements rather than competes with Bravi's lead capture — it adds the quoting step after the lead is captured.
